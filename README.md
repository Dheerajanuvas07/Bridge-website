# Bridge

> Non-medical medical appointment support for seniors in Lincoln, Nebraska.

Bridge helps independent seniors successfully complete medical appointments — door to door, start to finish — while giving their adult children certainty, accountability, and peace of mind.

---

## The Problem

Adult children managing aging parents face a specific, recurring crisis: getting their parent to, through, and home from a medical appointment without taking a full day off work.

Standard options fail them:

- **Rideshares** drop at the curb and drive away. They do not escort inside, help with check-in, stay through the visit, or confirm the appointment completed.
- **Home care agencies** require contracts, minimum hours, and ongoing commitments. They are designed for sustained in-home care, not one-time appointment support.
- **Family members** absorb the burden. Half-days of PTO. Working from waiting rooms. Coordinating from across the country.

The gap between "a ride" and "full home care" is where Bridge operates.

---

## The Solution

Bridge is a non-medical appointment execution service. A Bridge Captain picks up the senior at home, escorts them inside the clinic, supports check-in, waits through the entire appointment, brings them home safely, and sends a confirmation report to the authorized family contact.

Bridge is not a ride service. It is not a medical service. It is appointment completion — reliable, accountable, and reported.

**What Bridge Captains do:**
- Pre-appointment confirmation
- Pickup from home
- Walk-in escort
- Check-in support
- Waiting during the appointment
- Safe return home
- Bridge Completion Report to authorized family contact

**What Bridge Captains do not do:**
- Medical advice, clinical interpretation, or treatment decisions
- Medication administration or prescription handling
- Physical lifting or transfer assistance
- Emergency medical transport — in an emergency, Captains call 911

## Pricing Philosophy

### Why flat pricing

Families booking Bridge are often managing elevated emotional and logistical load. The last thing they need is anxiety about whether a long appointment will spike a bill. Flat pricing eliminates bill uncertainty and is the psychologically correct structure for a trust-based service.

"Will I get charged more if the appointment runs long?" is a question that, once asked, erodes trust. Flat pricing prevents the question from existing.

### Why $89 and not less

Price signals trust in care-adjacent services. This is counterintuitive compared to normal consumer markets — but when a service involves a vulnerable loved one, low price can generate doubt rather than conversions. "Why is this so cheap? Are these people vetted? What corners are being cut?"

$89 sits in the professional service range for Lincoln, Nebraska. It is below the psychological "$100 resistance threshold" for first-time buyers, while clearly above the "suspiciously casual" zone. It is also operationally necessary: a Captain working a 3.5-hour appointment block at competitive Lincoln wages requires pricing that covers the work.

### Why subscriptions improve operational reliability

Predictable volume from monthly subscriptions enables route planning, consistent Captain scheduling, and better scheduling density. Two efficiently coordinated appointments per Captain per day is operationally sustainable. Ad-hoc single bookings with poor scheduling density are not.

Subscriptions convert Bridge from a transactional service into infrastructure — something families stop thinking about and start relying on. That shift matters for retention, Captain continuity, and long-term operational health.

### Why same-Captain continuity matters

A senior who knows "Margaret is coming" is receiving something categorically different from a stranger in a car. Captain continuity reduces anxiety, builds trust, and improves the quality of each appointment interaction over time. It is the highest-value feature Bridge can offer — and it is locked behind Reserved Care and monthly plans, which creates a real reason to move up from one-time bookings.

## Market

**Current geography:** Lincoln, Nebraska  
**Primary customer:** Adult children (roughly age 38–58) coordinating care for aging parents  
**Secondary customer:** The senior's trusted contact network — spouses, siblings, close friends

The Lincoln market is characterized by strong trust economics — word of mouth travels fast and carries significant weight. One exceptional experience creates referral behavior that would cost hundreds of dollars in CAC through paid acquisition. One bad experience surfaces at church, at the school pickup, at the grocery store. Pricing and Captain quality are not separable from reputation in this market.
